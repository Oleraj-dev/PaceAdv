Background

Capitalizing on a recent rezoning and confidence in the growth and potential of Downtown Brooklyn, two venerable New York real estate families – Rose Associates and Benenson Capital – partnered to develop a luxury, ground-up, residential high-rise. The joint venture engaged us to name the building and develop and execute a full suite of marketing materials. Employing a strategic approach, and after significant conversations with the development team, we conceived the Hoy & Horn brand to resonate with the target millennial audience, stand out amid the noise of DoBro’s crowded marketplace, and define the building’s persona, vibe, and lifestyle.

Challenge

Appeal to millennial and younger audiences in an increasingly competitive residential real estate market. With the rapid growth of luxury high-rises and urban development, our marketing needed to stand out—targeting young, successful urbanites with a sense of luxury that breaks away from the sterile feel of typical glass towers. Why Brooklyn? Why here, and why now?

The Solution

Developing a Brooklyn-centric focus was the throughline for various marketing tools to tell the Hoyt & Horn story. Critical authenticity was reflected in an Instagram-inspired video, a distinctive letterpress brochure, and a multi-media leasing center. The campaign was executed in a scrapbook style to provide visual distinction and deliver the message of the power of creating one’s life.

The combination of a winning strategy and a differentiated graphic style contributed to significant leasing success from opening day. Our client was equally pleased with both the product and process.
