Background

Our client acquired and entitled 1.2 miles of waterfront land along the Harlem River in the Fordham section of The Bronx, New York. After a four-year process they were eager to begin marketing a 350,000 RSF Class A community building in the first phase of an extensive mixed-use development. Our team explored a variety of naming options for full development based on its location, historic significance and future vision for the neighborhood. The name Fordham Landing was selected for the overall development with the community building designated 1 Fordham Landing.

Challenge

The challenge was multi-fold. First, the project, located in an underdeveloped area of The Bronx was not on the radar of brokers and prospective tenants. Second, there were misperceptions about the area, which needed to be overcome. Lastly, understanding that the totality of the project would take shape over the better part of two decades, our work needed to educate the community, while feeling both fresh and timeless.

The Solution

We created an overall branding vision for the full mixed-use development which included the first commercial building to be constructed, 1 Fordham Landing. It was necessary to relate the two graphically. yet allow them each to carry its own distinction. In this process we were mindful of the next element, a two-tower multifamily building which would be coming to market within the next year. Drawing on the site’s location at the river’s edge, we developed a visual aesthetic that can adapt to many types of uses, integrate all the elements of the overall development and resonate with multiple audiences – initially commercial tenants and brokers, subsequently residential tenants and brokers, as well as long-time and newer neighborhood residents. The branding needed to feel inviting and welcoming as well as clean and professional to lend credibility to our client’s efforts.

Our client and their real estate leasing brokers are delighted with the design and storytelling evidenced in our branding campaign. We have successfully developed detailed leasing materials for the community building and vigorous leasing efforts are underway amid strong interest in the initial building.
